My Story
Somalia

Where it started

I grew up in Somalia — a country defined by insecurity, war, and limited opportunity. There was no tech scene, no startup culture, no path to follow. I finished high school but never collected the certificate. I didn't know what I wanted to be. I just knew I needed to leave.

2024

Arrived in Germany

I landed in Germany and for the first few months, everything felt possible. New country, new start, new energy. I was happy. Shopping with friends, exploring the city, feeling alive.

Then reality hit. I opened LinkedIn for the first time and saw people talking about technical skills, business achievements, certifications. I closed it and thought: "This is not my world. I don't belong here."

2024

The warehouse

My first job in Europe was in a warehouse. Six months of sorting glasses into categories. One day, a coworker told me: "You learn incredibly fast. Where did you work before?" I lied — because I was ashamed of my background. That lie ate me alive. I decided I'd never pretend again.

Then they asked me to train two new workers. I taught them the system — categories, locations, processes. And something clicked. I loved it. Not the warehouse — the teaching. Taking something I understood and making someone else understand it too.

2024

The train rides

After exhausting 8-hour shifts, I'd sit on the train home and study AI automation. The idea that you could connect systems together — that they could run without you — fascinated me. I was hooked. Every free minute went into learning how APIs talk to each other, how workflows chain, how AI makes decisions.

2025

The turning point

Warehouse work became automatic. My body worked while my mind reflected. One shift, it hit me: Why not teach? Why not build? Why not combine the two things I love — learning systems and sharing knowledge?

A friend once told me teachers don't make money. That stuck with me for years. But standing in that warehouse, I realized: you teach your children, you teach your team, you teach through what you build. Teaching isn't a job title — it's who you are.

I cried that day. Then I quit. I dedicated everything to AI, automation, and building systems that matter.

2025–2026

Building in public

Since then, I've built 12+ production systems. An autonomous LinkedIn engine that publishes 6 posts daily. A semantic memory brain with 796+ entries. 26 Trigger.dev tasks running 24/7. Monitoring dashboards. Job hunting pipelines. YouTube content. All self-taught. All real. All running.

I haven't made a cent from it yet. But I know what I can build, I know what I can teach, and I know the value compounds. I'm learning to package it. The systems are ready. So am I.
